<h1>Clean &amp; Green Singapore Day 2024</h1>
<h4>Launch of CleanPod and Clean-up at West Coast Park</h4>
<p>This yearâ€™s Clean and Green Singapore (CGS) Day is hosted by the South
West Community Development Council (SWCDC) and held in partnership with
National University of Singapore (NUS).</p>
<p>In support of the Year of Public Hygiene, Deputy Prime Minister (DPM)
Mr Heng Swee Keat together with Ms Low Yen Ling, Mayor of South West District
as well as Grassroots Leaders from West Coast Constituency and student
representatives from NUS, launched a CleanPod at West Coast Park. DPM Heng
also flagged off a park clean-up with over 70 participants, using tools
from the newly launched CleanPod.</p>

<h2>CGS Community Events 2024 </h2>
<h4>ECO Paddle with Ola</h4>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/CGS 2023/CGS 2023/Photo_1.jpg">
</div>
<p>On 13 October 2024, the Ola Beach Club and the National Environment Agency
(NEA) hosted the 'Eco Paddle with Ola' event at Siloso Beach in Sentosa.
Senior Parliamentary Secretary for the Ministry of Sustainability and the
Environment, Mr. Baey Yam Keng, joined the community to foster a sense
of belonging and unity in preserving the cleanliness of public spaces.</p>
<p>More than 100 participants actively engaged in kayak litter-picking in
the surrounding waters and cleaned up the shoreline, enjoying a meaningful
Sunday morning on the island.</p>
<p>We extend special thanks to our partners and sponsors who greatly contributed
to the event's success: Coca-Cola Singapore, Ministry of Manpower-Friends
of ACE (FACE), Mount Faber Leisure Group, NEA Volunteer Corps, Public Hygiene
Council, Sentosa Development Corporation, Singapore Paddle Club, and Soffell
Singapore.</p>
